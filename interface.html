iOS distribution settings

Before your app can be distributed, we need some additional information. Select the tab(s) that match how you want to distribute your app and add the data required. Once you submit your information and assets, we will process your request and distribute your app in your prefered ways within 2 business days. Apple may take up to 5 days to review apps.

For more information about these fields and examples, see Apple's official recommendations.

2 - App Store listing details (Required)

All information you put in below will be publicly visible on your app's listing on the App Store.

The name below the icon on the home screen.

The length of your app icon name is limited to 13 characters

A summary of your app that will appear under your app’s name on the App Store for customers with devices running iOS 11 or later - Optional

Subtitles are limited to 30 characters.

Provide an engaging description that highlights the benefits and functionality of your app. Only first sentence will be visible on the App Store without expanding.

Descriptions are limited from 10 to 4000 characters.

Your app’s promotional text appears at the top of the description - Optional

Your app’s promotional text is limited to 170 characters.

The category that best describes this app. For more information, see the App Store Category Definitions.

The secondary category that best describes this app. For more information, see the App Store Category Definitions.

The secondary category can not be the same as the primary category.

Keywords help determine where your app displays in search results.

Type a keyword and press enter or enter a comma. Keywords are limited to 100 characters total.

Your app will be set to free app by default. If you want to change it to a paid app, please get in touch with us.

Your apps' recommended audience age

Your app rating is determined by Apple based on your app type. The app rating will be displayed on the App Store across all your platforms. Fliplet apps will be rated 17+ by default as it means an app can load web pages and youtube videos.

A URL that links to your organization's support page.

A URL that links to your organization's marketing website.

A URL that links to your organization's privacy policy.

The name of the person or entity that owns the exclusive rights to your app, preceded by the year the rights were obtained.
